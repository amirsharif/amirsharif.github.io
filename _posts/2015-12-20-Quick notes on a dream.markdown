Had two visually interesting dreams last night.


# 1- A Gallery and a Garden

I was visiting a gallery or a shop of some sort with a group of people (I think I was there with my sister but I'm not sure anymore). When we approached the building, we met with a guide who picked us up from the driveway and took us to an entrance tucked inside an alley. From the outside, there were sandstone walls about 5 or 6 feet high around the perimeter and they were topped with a series of crushed glass screens that added an additional 4 or 5 feet of height. The screens were about 8 feet wide and curved into 90-degree bends around the edges, with 1-foot gaps in between where thin sycamore trees were placed (they were planted inside the perimeter of the wall). Everything was a sort of yellow-beige like someone had put a permanent Valencia filter on the complex.

As we walked around this outside wall, you could occasionally see gimpses of the interior between the trees and glass screens -- there was a hint of a plain water fountain spouting a single stream and a plain building behind a small garden. I could also hear the sound of birds and people eating just over the wall.

I don't have clear memories of entering but there was a long, dark corridor that led to the garden. The interior of the building was in the same color scheme and made of stone/marble -- it looked like a restored Modernist building. The corridor ended in wide rectangle that led to the garden. It was a very bright entrance. There were broad sliding doors that had been pushed open. We walked into the garden -- down a few short steps -- and I could see a long fountain that had a Giacommetti man placed in the pool near one end and a single water spout shooting roughly to the same height as the sculpture on the opposite end. There were plain outdoor tables set on a pinkish-sandstone gravel under the shade of the sycamore trees. Old women in big glasses were having lunch. Waiter were appearing from and disappearing into the side of the building, like the kitchen in Manuscript. The building was a 2-story building with a plain balcony extending across the second floor. I think in hindsight the scene was an amalgamation of what I imagine my ideal retail shop to look like, made of elements of houses and schools in Tehran and the Manuscript building (which looks and feels very similar given the Modernist elements).

# 2- An Airport?

The last dream I had before waking up was a very vivid space, though I'm not sure exactly what purpose it served. I was there alone but there was a big crowd of people racing and talking and being generally busy. It felt like maybe an airport or a department store during a holiday rush.

I didn't see the outside of the building -- I had just arrived inside. The interior was an enormous, white rectangular space. There were no columns. Just huge and white. The floors where also white but there were plush black carpets and runners identifying paths and marking off areas where people should stand. Everything had a right angle and was rectangular -- generally following what looked like the golden ratio.

The space was marked off by two types of blocks:

1. Tall vitrines that showed stylized scenes of american suburban life in monochromes (a boy walking his dog against a row of houses all in deep shades of blues; a little maquette of the painted ladies in deep greens). These vitrines had bright white bases that came up about 4 feet and 1-inch-by-1-inch thick white frames around the vitrines that added an additional 2 feet or so of height (extensions of the bases). The white bases and frames were matte but the scenes had a glossy depth to them. In total these vitrines were about 6 feet tall and marked off the larger space into areas that were public vs. areas that were restricted (what I imagine would have been airport security/paths to the gates).

2. Airport counters. These were made of black desks that looked like they were made of glass (very reflective but have a strange depth, like an iPhone screen). On the desks -- sort of placed as decorative bookends -- were strange little glass and white marble sculptures that looked like beakers and flasks and little conical shapes encased in glass cylinders. They didn't have a practical purpose as far as I could tell. Some of them looked like they were spinning fast and others looked like they had maybe some liquid in them. Behind the counters were people in white coats but who were not scientists -- they looked like Kiehl's salespeople. Behind them were tall digital screens on white bases (the same white bases as the vitrines). The screens were about 4 feet tall and completely black, with very thin white lines creating shapes and messages on them (a thin white line would be drawn into a circle then disappear again -- like a loading screen) and thin white letters spelled out different messages, which I don't remember. 

Above, the larger space extended into the air for what felt like 10 stories, ending in a perfectly plain, flat, white ceiling dotted with rows of lights. I saw a glimpse of a corridor stretching out of one side of the rectangular hall -- it was a narrower entrance, with the ceiling and walls jutting into the corridor's entrance (e.g. if the side of the rectangular box I was in measured 200 feet by 300 feet, the corridor began as a 150 feet by 250 feet opening).

I think the colors and images were actually drawn from an Instagram photo I saw taken by a friend [(@frequentlyaskedquestion)][instalink] of a photography exhibition at MoMA. Feel relieved now that I've identified the references: Probably a combination of seeing those imagines and my white iPhone with its black screen, all extended out into an airport terminal.
